Phishing Awareness Training Module

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack where attackers pose as trusted individuals or organizations to trick users into revealing sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal details. These attacks are often delivered through email, text, or fake websites that look legitimate. Phishing can lead to identity theft, data breaches, and financial loss.

Email Phishing

Email phishing is the most common form of phishing, where attackers send emails that appear to be from legitimate sources such as banks, coworkers, or service providers. These messages often contain urgent language, suspicious links, or attachments that prompt users to take immediate action. Falling for these emails can give attackers access to your accounts or personal data.

Spear Phishing

Spear phishing is a more targeted form of phishing that uses personal details to make the message seem legitimate. Attackers may know your name, job title, or recent activity, making it harder to detect. These emails are often more convincing and designed to steal login credentials or install malware.

Smishing and Vishing

Smishing uses text messages (SMS) to trick recipients into clicking malicious links, downloading attachments, or revealing sensitive information. Vishing occurs over voice calls, where attackers pretend to be tech support, banks, or government officials. Both methods rely on urgency and fear to quickly manipulate victims.

Emerging Threats: AI-Powered and Deepfake Phishing

AI-generated phishing uses machine learning to create more realistic and personalized messages. Deepfake phishing takes this further by using synthetic audio or video to impersonate real individuals, such as a CEO or coworker. These advanced tactics are highly convincing and difficult to detect without proper training and awareness.

Warning: If a message or video seems “off,” trust your instincts and verify the sender using a known contact method before reacting.

Common Red Flags of Phishing

Recognizing phishing attempts often comes down to spotting red flags. These include:

  • Unfamiliar or misspelled email addresses: e.g., IT-Support@micr0soft.com
  • Unexpected attachments or links: e.g., a zip file from someone you don’t know
  • Urgent or threatening language: e.g., "Your account will be locked in 24 hours!"
  • Requests for sensitive information: e.g., being asked to confirm your login or Social Security number
  • Poor grammar or formatting: e.g., "Dear user, You account has been susspended. Click hear to verify."

Being able to identify these signs is essential for avoiding scams and protecting your personal and sensitive information.

Tip: Always check the sender's email address and avoid clicking on unexpected links. Hover over links before clicking to preview where they lead!

How to Report Phishing Attacks at Five Guys

If you believe you've received a phishing email while working at Five Guys, it's important to take action quickly. While the exact internal reporting contact may vary, you should always follow standard corporate security practices to help protect yourself and your team.

Here are the recommended steps to report a phishing email:

  • Step 1: Check your Outlook or email client for a “Report Phishing” button or plugin. Many corporate environments use these tools to make reporting suspicious emails quick and easy.
  • Step 2: Review your employee handbook or IT security policies, as they often include company-specific instructions for reporting suspicious activities.
  • Step 3: If no internal method is available, use one of these general reporting options:
    • Forward the phishing email to reportphishing@apwg.org.
    • Report the message to your email provider (e.g., Gmail, Outlook).
    • Submit the phishing attempt to the FTC at ReportFraud.ftc.gov.

⚠️ Important: Do not click on any links or download any attachments in the suspicious email. Report it first, then delete the message once you've submitted it to the proper channel.
